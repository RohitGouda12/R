Areca nut

Areca nut is the seed of a palm tree, commonly known as betel nut, and is chewed as a mild stimulant, often with betel leaves, spices, and slaked lime. It is a tropical crop with commercial importance in many parts of Asia, and its name is even used in some place names, such as Penang Island in Malaysia.

Uses of areca nut:

  • Areca nut palm is used for timber and fuel wood.
  • The nut and plant are sources of a natural dye.
  • It has been used in traditional Indian medicine for various ailments.
  • The most common use is chewing the areca nut, often with betel leaf and other ingredients, as a mild stimulant.

Care of areca nut

Climate: Cashews thrive in tropical coastal regions with moderate rainfall and temperatures between 20°C and 35°C.

Planting: Young saplings are transplanted to orchards after 12-18 months. They need abundant sunlight and well-drained, slightly acidic soil.

Harvest & Processing: Cashew apples are picked, the nuts detached and sun-dried. The nuts have a toxic, caustic resin (cashew nut shell liquid - CNSL) between two shells, so they must be roasted or steamed to remove the liquid and make shelling safe and feasible. The kernels are then peeled, graded, and packed, often using inert gas like carbon dioxide to prevent infestation.

Climate & Soil: Prefers a warm, humid climate with filtered sunlight or full sun exposure. It needs deep, well-draining, loamy soil.

Watering: Requires regular irrigation and cannot withstand drought.

Maintenance: Pruning is done in the early stages, and the plant has good resistance to pests if cared for properly.
